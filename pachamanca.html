Pachamanca

Ancient Andean Earth Oven Cooking

What is Pachamanca?

Pachamanca is an ancient Peruvian cooking method from pre-Columbian times, when the Incas lived in this region. "Pachamanca" comes from Quechua (an indigenous language) and means "pot of the earth."

Image: Javi270270, CC BY-SA 3.0, Wikimedia Commons

How Pachamanca Works

1 Dig a hole in the ground
2 Heat stones over fire until very hot
3 Place hot stones in the hole

Image: Georgina B. Vega C., CC BY-SA 4.0, Wikimedia Commons

How Pachamanca Works

4 Layer foods on stones (meats, potatoes, corn)
5 Cover with hot stones, leaves, and earth
6 Food cooks slowly underground, creating amazing flavors!

Special Andean Ingredients

Pachamanca uses traditional Andean foods:

  • Habas: Large fava beans that grow in mountains
  • Ollucos: Colorful tubers with yellow or pink flesh
  • Choclos: Large-kernel Andean corn

People Making Pachamanca

Pachamanca is a communal cooking tradition that brings people together. Preparing and sharing this meal strengthens community bonds and preserves indigenous cultural practices.
